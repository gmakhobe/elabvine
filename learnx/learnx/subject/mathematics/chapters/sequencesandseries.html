Sequences

Arithmetic Sequence
An arithmetic sequence is a sequence where consecutive terms are calculated by adding a constant value (positive or negative) to the previous term. We call this constant value the common difference (d).
For example : ×

Step 1
Sequence of
-4; 0; 4; 8; 12...

Step 2
You find common difference
T2 - T1 = T3 - T2 = d
practically as 0T2 - (-4T1) = 4T3 - 0T2 = 4d

∴ If we keep adding 4 to each term we get the next term.

If we use a for the first term T1, d for the common difference, then the general term Tn for an arithmetic sequence is: Tn = a + (n – 1)d
×
Tn = a + (n − 1)d
where:
  • Tn is the nth term.
  • n is the position of the term in the sequence.
  • a is the first term.
  • d is the common difference.
Quadratic Sequence
A quadratic sequence is a sequence of numbers in which the second difference between any two consecutive terms is constant.
× For Example :

Step 1
Sequence of
6, 12, 22, 36, 64...

Step 2
Find your first difference
12t2 - 6t1 = 6
22t3 - 12t2 = 10
36t4 - 22t3 = 14
54t5 - 36t4 = 18

Step 3
From First difference find Second Common difference
Which is
10 - 4 = 18 - 14 = 4

step 4
Second common difference is 4
Here we find the value of c
So 2a = 4
∴ a = 2

step 5
Here we find the value of b
T2 – T1 = 8
So 3a + b = 8
∴ 3(2) + b = 8
∴ b = 2

step 6
First term is 12
So a + b + c = 12
∴ 2 + 2 + c = 12
∴ c = 8

General Formula
∴Tn = 2n2 + 2n + 8

Geometric Sequence
A geometric sequence is a sequence of numbers in which each new term (except for the first term) is calculated by multiplying the previous term by a constant value called the constant ratio (r).
× For Example :

Step 1
Sequence of
2, 4, 8, 16, 32...

Step 2
Find common ratio : t2 ⁄ t1 = r
4 ⁄ 2 = 2
8 ⁄ 4 = 2
16 ⁄ 8 = 2
32 ⁄ 16 = 2

General Formula
T n = ar n − 1

where:
  • a is the first term in the sequence.
  • r is the constant ratio.

Step 3
From general formula substitute a and r
T n = 2(2)n − 1

Series

Arithmetic and Geometric Series
When we add the terms of a sequence together, we form a series. We use the symbol Sn to show the sum of the first n terms of a series.

So Sn = T1 + T2 + T3 + T4 + … + Tn
Arithmetic Series
× The formula is Sn = n ⁄ 2 [2a + (n − 1)d] where Sn is the sum of n terms,

a is the first term,
n is the number of terms and
d is the common difference.

Geometric Series
× The formula is Sn = a(rn − 1) ⁄ r − 1 for r > 1 or Sn = a(1 - rn) ⁄ 1 - r for r 1 where Sn is the sum of n terms,

a is the first term
r is the common ratio
n is the number of terms
Sn is the sum of the terms
